Exploring Cappadocia, Turkey

2019 Fall | Group Work | Instructor: Matthew Seibert
Software: Autodesk Recap, Unity | Group Member: Xinyu Tu, Zheyu Liu

The magnificent landscape in Cappadocia is decided by the unique characteristic of ground material there. The fairy chimneys and terraces are “sculpted” by environmental phenomena-sunlight, wind, rainfall, etc. They shaped the landform into its current condition, and will also erase what we have now in the future.
We cannot prevent the landform from eroding, but we can keep the sediments in the site. With different installation, we can intercept sediments carried by different weather conditions-- taller and curved installations for sediments in wind, and lower installation for those carried in surface runoff. When sediments accumulated at those points, it will develop into new landforms, and can even be manipulated by the position of installations. In this case, we can create a nice landscape which is also a friendly habitat for non-human creatures.
