Interweaving the Valley

Sharing Land Tenure in Californiaâ€™s Central Valley

2020 Spring | Group Work
Group Members: Xinyu Tu, Junhong Fu, Tian Wang
Instructors: Bradley Cantrell, Andrea Hansen, Michael Ezban

Land tenure reveals resource injustices. The distribution of farming resources is significantly unbalanced between farms. This project sits deeply within social structures surrounding the practice of farming. We are committed to enhancing social equity within the agricultural context in Central Valley. We believe that low-income working groups ought to have adequate living support. Minority farmers, including the Hmong people who have been somehow regionally and socially isolated, can become an integrated part of the farm landscape. On the other hand, their practices may also bring changes to the existing farming condition in Central Valley and benefit the environment.

We did some experiments on how nitrogen might be transferred in the field. we first tested how nitrogen can be carried through runoff and concentrated if they've been inserted into large farmlands, and then applied them into some structures, including housing and bridges, to provide some infrastructures for local farmers while serving as nitrogen distributors.
