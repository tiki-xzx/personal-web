Sunset Encounter

2019 Fall | Individual Work
Instructor: Beth Meyer

Located in a parking lot, the seating place is an enigmatic attractor in this “unpopular” place for visitors. On Saturday mornings, it serves as a screen between the market and the parking garage. Every evening, as the sun sets, this west-facing stepped platform provides a spot for a spectacular view of sunsets and western skies over the “skyline” of Second Street.

Sedimenting Memories

2019 Fall | Group Work | Instructor: Beth Meyer
Group Members: Xinyu Tu, Binyu Yang, Jiajing Lyu, Jingjing Ji, Leah Kahler

In deploying water as conveyor of memory, water as space maker, and materials as registers of wetness, our framework plan seeks to reveal existing natureculture hybrids along second street, excavating the experience of Charlottesville as a river city with continuously contested social histories of racialized topography. We embrace the partial, incomplete, and contested natures of memory and urban water.
This project responds to the constructed division between upper second street and lower second street. The material and spatial qualities of the current condition render the experience of second street above the tracks dry and selectively “historical,” and that below the tracks, humid and void of visible markers of memory. The North is oversaturated with monuments and brick, while the literal urban ground is oversaturated below the tracks.

Transporting matter and memories

From a 20th century Car park to a Mobility park for the 21st century

2019 Fall | Individual Work | Instructor: Beth Meyer

This project is located on a one acre sloping parking lot located on the edge between Charlottesville’s nineteenth century historic district centered on the Mall and a 25-acre zone demolished by urban renewal in the early 1970s. I propose to create a new public space on this block comprised of two wide public promenades connecting the lower income neighborhoods to the south to downtown as well as support facilities for multimodal mobility systems. It attends to the long-term environmental impacts of this automobile landscapes, and its petrochemical by-products.
