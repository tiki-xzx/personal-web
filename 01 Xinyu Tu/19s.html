Farming Toxins

Catalyzing Remediation through Community Engagement

2019 Spring | Individual Work | Instructor: Matthew Seibert

In Oakland, due to urban development and historical reasons, a large portion of soil has been polluted, which has a great influence on food production. Through a series of investigation on resources of pollution, corresponding remediation can be applied to solve those existing problems. Meanwhile, some part of the land with least pollution will serve as urban farms and planted with fast growing edible plants to show the contrast in time between food production and soil remediation and call for people’s awareness of environmental protection. Local residents and children will be joining the whole process. Professional gardeners will be guiding them while taking charge of some of the public space that local people will not be working on, such as industrial area. After being trained, some residents will take gardeners’ place and be responsible for most of the farming work.
