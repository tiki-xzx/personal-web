Phasing the Flood

A Retreat, Reconnection and Recollection Strategy for Shanghai

2020 Fall | Individual Work
Instructors: Missy Velez, Kathleen Adams

Built up at the tip of Yangtze River Delta, the location of Shanghai has brought the city both chances in port trade and risks of being flooded. While the available land is shrinking, the population is still increasing rapidly. Looking back into history, there has been a classic community typology in Shanghai called longtang, which was designed to fit the high population density.

However, in recent decades, a lot of longtang communities has been replaced by skyscrapers. Meanwhile, current flood control infrastructures are not taking permanent future flood into consideration. This project is proposing a plan for phased flooding at regional scale with the new community typology derived from traditional longtang community for people to relocate, reconnect and recollect the memory of the city.
